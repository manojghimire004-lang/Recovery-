🌅 Morning Session
Ankle Alphabet
A→Z with big toe · 2 rounds · Both feet
▼
1
Sit in a chair. Lift your foot completely off the floor.
2
Use your big toe as a pen. Write every letter A to Z in the air — make the letters BIG. Move your whole ankle, not just your toe.
3
Go slowly. Feel your ankle rotating in every direction.
4
Switch feet. Do 2 full rounds on each foot.
Why this works
Forces your ankle through every plane of motion — up, down, left, right, diagonal. Your calcaneus controls side-to-side movement and this directly retrains it.
Towel Scrunches
3 sets × 10 reps · Each foot
▼
1
Place a small towel flat on the floor in front of your chair.
2
Put your bare foot on the towel. Sit up straight.
3
Curl your toes downward, gripping and pulling the towel toward you. Hold 2 seconds, then release flat.
4
10 scrunches = 1 set. Rest 30 seconds between sets. Do 3 sets per foot.
Why this works
Strengthens the intrinsic arch muscles that directly support the calcaneus when you walk. Weak arch = your heel takes more impact = more pain and limp.
Calf Raises
3 × 10 reps · Slow · Hold chair
▼
1
Stand behind a chair. Hold the back lightly — just for balance, don't lean on it.
2
Slowly rise up onto your toes — count 2 seconds going UP.
3
Hold at the top for 1 second. Feel the stretch.
4
Slowly lower back down — count 3 seconds going DOWN. This slow descent is the most important part.
5
10 reps = 1 set. Rest 45 seconds. Do 3 sets. Week 3+: try single leg (injured foot only).
Why this works
Your Achilles tendon attaches directly to the calcaneus. Weak calves = you compensate by limping. This is the #1 exercise to fix your gait.
Heel Walks
2 minutes · Toes always up · Around room
▼
1
Stand up straight near a wall (in case you need balance).
2
Lift the front of both feet off the ground. Toes pointing UP in the air.
3
Walk around your room using ONLY your heels. Go slowly and controlled.
4
Keep toes up the entire time. Do this for 2 full minutes without stopping.
Why this works
Directly loads your calcaneus bone with controlled weight. Builds bone density and strengthens the muscles around the fracture site. You'll feel it working immediately.
Single Leg Balance
30 sec × 3 · Injured foot · Most important!
▼
1
Stand near a wall or hold a chair lightly with one finger.
2
Lift your GOOD foot off the ground. Balance on your INJURED foot only.
3
Hold for 30 seconds. Rest 20 seconds. Repeat 3 times.
4
Week 2: Try with eyes closed. Week 4: Try standing on a folded blanket (unstable surface).
⚡ Most important exercise for your limp
After 2 years, your brain learned to avoid trusting your injured foot. This exercise directly retrains your nervous system to trust it again. This is why you limp — not just the bone.
